## PAGES 3-4: THE NAPKIN STORY
### *From Amazon's Flywheel to Knowledge's Flywheel*

> *"All great movements begin with a simple sketch."*

### The Original Napkin (1997)

In a Seattle apartment, Jeff Bezos drew a circle on a napkin:

```
     Customer Experience
            ↓
         Traffic
            ↓
         Sellers
            ↓
       Selection
            ↓
      Lower Prices
            ↓
   (back to Customer Experience)
```

**This became Amazon.**

Not because the idea was complex.  
But because it was *self-reinforcing*.  
Every turn made the next turn easier.  
Every customer attracted more sellers.  
Every seller expanded selection.  
Every selection lowered prices.  
Every price improvement delighted customers.

**A virtuous cycle.**

### The Knowledge Napkin (2025)

What if we drew a new circle?

```
     📚 Knowledge Creation
            ↓
     💡 Tax Incentives for Learning
            ↓
     🌍 More Authors, Scientists, Educators
            ↓
     🔄 Revenue Reinvested in Ecosystem
            ↓
     📈 More People Buy, Learn, Contribute
            ↓
   (back to Knowledge Creation — stronger)
```

**This becomes The Tax Book.**

Not because tax is exciting.  
But because *intentional tax policy* can be the engine of human liberation.

### The Missing Link

Amazon's flywheel worked because:
✅ Every participant benefited

**Knowledge has no such flywheel.**

- Authors write → but 99% never find readers

**What if we connected them?**

### *The Teacher Who Filed Taxes for a Library*

> *In 2019, Priya Mehta, a primary school teacher in rural Gujarat, India, earned ₹28,000/month ($340).*

Every April, Priya filed a "Nil Return" — a formal declaration that she earned too little to owe tax.
